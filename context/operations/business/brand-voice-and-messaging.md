## Voice & Tone

### How We Sound

- **Direct, not corporate.** No fluff. Say what you mean.
- **Helpful, not salesy.** We're giving them something, not pitching them.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** We know what we're talking about. We don't need to oversell it.
- **Warm, not formal.** We're a community. Write like a person, not a company.
- **Transparent.** Not everything wins. We don't pretend otherwise. Losses are part of the process â€” we use them as learning opportunities.

## Messaging by Context

### Welcome Message (New Member DM)

**Goal:** Make them feel seen, orient them to what DD is, and get them to claim the trial â€” without overwhelming them.

**Lead with:** Who we are (education-first, not picks). Then: what they get (tools, community, strategies). Then: the trial offer with no friction.

**Tone:** Warm and direct. One human talking to another. Not a blast email.

**What to avoid:** Long lists of features. Jargon. Emojis overload. Anything that reads like a sales pitch.

### Welcome Ticket (In-Server Thread)

**Goal:** Get them to claim All Access. Secondary goal: start a conversation.

**Lead with:** The gift (free days, no strings). Then: what they unlock. Keep it short.

**Tone:** Even more casual than the DM. They're already in the server.

### Partner Introductions / EV Channel Descriptions

**Goal:** Explain WHY a tool is valuable, not just that it exists.

**The model:**
> "[App name] is useful because [specific reason]. I wouldn't use them for [X], but they're the best for [Y]."

Never: "Sign up for [App] and use our promo code." Always lead with the educational reason.

### Staff Interactions with Members

**Goal:** Help members make more educated decisions.

**The model:** Add context, add factors, ask questions back. Don't just answer â€” teach.

**Staff should never:** Pressure members to subscribe. Recommend a bet without explaining the reasoning.
