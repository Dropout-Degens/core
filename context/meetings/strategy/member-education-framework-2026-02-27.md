## The 6 Core Principles

### 1. Bankroll and Unit Sizing

**The rule:** 1-3% of total bankroll per unit. Never exceed 3-5 units in a day.

**Why it works:**
- Forces self-limiting on volume (you can only pick the 3-5 highest conviction plays)
- Survives variance — a 5-unit day against a $1,000 bankroll is $50-$150 max loss
- Unit size is your speed governor — change it slowly, change it deliberately

**The error most people make:**
> "I bet $20 normally but it's the Celtics, so I'm putting $150 on it."

That's not a unit system. That's emotions with extra steps.

**What $0.25 units mean:** Not a small bettor. A disciplined bettor. If your bankroll constrains you, that's the correct unit. Don't oversize to feel like a serious bettor.

### 2. Variance is Real — Build for It

**Variance is not bad luck. It is math that will occur.**

The 90% coin flip analogy:
> "If I had a coin that lands heads 90% of the time, and I could bet as much as I wanted and flip as many times as I wanted — how much do I put on the first flip? All of it? Why not? It's 90/10."

Answer: because 10% happens. And 10% on your whole bankroll is catastrophic. The bet sizing should account for the 10%, not ignore it because the headline number is 90.

**Applied to picks:**
- A 70% confident pick still loses 30% of the time
- A full unit on every 70% pick will hit a losing streak by math
- Variance on any single day can look terrible — that's not the signal
- Signal is track over 100+ plays, not 10

**Two types of variance to manage:**
1. **Event variance** — one unexpected factor changes the market (injury, weather, ref, etc.)
2. **Capital variance** — placing more bets adds more independent variance events
   
**The compounding risk:**
> The more bets you place, the more variance events you are exposed to. They compound downward just as they compound upward.

**Daily approach:**
- Check: am I over my unit cap for the day?
- Check: is there a factor in this game I haven't fully researched?
- Check: is this bet actually adding value or am I just action-seeking?

### 3. The Time-Value of Research

**The concept:** Research time is a finite daily resource. Every bet you place was funded by research time. The ratio of research time to units wagered should be consistent.

**Two hours of research at 2 PM → 2 high-conviction bets at 1 unit each.**

Then at 6:30 PM — a game tips in 30 minutes and you throw $100 on it after reading a tweet.

That's not a bet. That's a donation.

**The formula isn't math — it's discipline:**

> Can I do this market justice with the time I have left in the day? If no, skip it. There will be another game tomorrow.

**The long-term framing:**
- Betting is a long-term game. Every game you skip because you couldn't research it is not a missed opportunity — it's a discipline win.
- There will always be something to bet on tomorrow.
- The only way to stay profitable over time is to only bet on the markets you've earned the right to bet on (via research).

**Actionable:**
- Set a research time budget each day before you start
- Stop picking bets when your research time is exhausted
- Never bet within 30 minutes of a game start without at least 90 minutes of prior research on that specific market

### 4. Diminishing Returns on Volume

**The counterintuitive truth:** Your best day betting is often your first or second win. Every bet after that adds more variance exposure than expected return.

**The 100% day:**
> "If you hit one bet today, you're at 100% win rate. Why would you risk placing another? If you lose, you're at 50%. If you win, you go to 66%. The question is whether those next bets are worth the risk to your daily equity."

**This isn't saying "stop after one win."** It's saying: evaluate each additional bet with fresh math, not with "I'm hot" energy.

**Analysts sending too many picks:**
- 10 picks/day from an analyst sounds like value
- For most members, it's rope
- Solution: treat it as a menu, not a shopping list
- Pick the 3-5 you have conviction in, size at half-unit each → stays under daily unit cap

### 5. Straights Over Parlays

**Parlays are a mechanism, not a strategy.**

**What a parlay actually is:** You win Bet A, and you put ALL of that money on Bet B at those odds. The sportsbook does this for you and presents it as a feature. It's not.

**Why straights win long-term:**
- Each bet is independent (variance doesn't compound)
- You win unit by unit and build consistent upward trend
- You can hedge, exit, or adjust each play individually
- Losses are singular — not catastrophic cascade

**Why parlays lose long-term:**
- Variance on one leg kills the whole ticket
- The more legs, the more variance events you're exposed to
- The big win is rare; the near-miss (hit 4 of 5) is frequent and demoralizing
- Sportsbooks price parlay legs with juice — you're overpaying vs. straights

**Exception: DFS apps (Flif, PrizePicks, etc.)**
- Forced parlay structure (2+ legs minimum)
- Line is typically set at +100 — no juice paid
- Two-leg plays on DFS = acceptable, because the structural odds are better
- Still: fewer legs is better on DFS too. Not 8-leg goblin tickets.

**The question to ask before any parlay:**
> "Would I bet these each individually? If yes, why am I combining them and introducing joint variance?"

### 6. Live Betting: Better Entry, Better Price

**The concept:** Lines are set by the market. Markets move on events. Events create temporary mispricing. Patience = better price.

**The Hurricane/Blackhawk example:**
- Carolina Hurricanes win the game (high confidence)
- But Blackhawks score first (moderate confidence)
- Strategy: wait for Blackhawks to score → Carolina line moves from -200 to +100
- Now bet Carolina at +100 instead of -200
  
**The return math:**
- $100 at -200 → win $50
- $50 at +100 → win $50 (same return, half the risk)

**This is live betting as a capital efficiency tool, not entertainment.**

**The principle applied everywhere:**
- Bucks at -8.5 spread? Wait for a run. Maybe it shifts to -2.5.
- If it never gets there, you saved yourself the bet. That's also a win.
- The shoe market comparison: shoes drop at $200, wait for $150. You gamble they won't go to $300 — but you're making a more informed decision.

**Important note:** This is advanced-level betting. Requires:
- Understanding of the market (why the line is where it is)
- Confidence in the eventual outcome
- Discipline to not bet just because you've been watching

## The Underlying Mental Model

Every bet is an allocation of two finite resources:
1. **Research time** (confidence earned)
2. **Capital** (risk taken)

The goal is to maximize the ratio of confidence earned per dollar risked.

That ratio gets worse as you:
- Add more bets (research time depletes)
- Bet larger (capital at risk grows)
- Parlay (variance compounds)
- Bet without research (confidence = 0)

That ratio gets better as you:
- Bet fewer, higher-conviction plays
- Stay within the unit cap
- Research thoroughly before committing
- Use live lines to improve entry price

## Responsible Gaming Note

This framework is for long-term profitability. It is not a guarantee. Even systematic, disciplined bettors lose money over short windows.

**What this framework does:**
- Prevents catastrophic drawdown
- Protects bankroll through losing streaks
- Forces selectivity (only your best bets)
- Teaches how to evaluate markets, not just follow picks

**What it doesn't do:**
- Make you invincible against variance
- Turn gambling into a job overnight
- Replace independent research
- Guarantee profitability in any given week

**The responsible framing:**
- Only bet what you've already allocated to your betting bankroll
- Never chase a loss with a new bet made under stress
- Step away from betting when emotional
- Profit is measured over months, not days
