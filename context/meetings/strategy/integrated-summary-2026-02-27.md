# Integrated Strategy Summary — Feb 7 & 27, 2026

**Context:** Two strategy sessions conducted during the critical pre-launch phase for $50/month positioning and infrastructure overhaul.

---

## The Problem We're Solving

The core tension: DD has built something with real edge (18+ months of OddsJam data, EV methodology, analyst network), but **members aren't profitable because they don't understand how to deploy capital responsibly.**

When analysts hit 60%+ on picks, members fail because they:
- Follow all picks instead of sizing appropriately
- Don't understand variance and downswings
- Over-leverage on single events
- Parlay everything instead of playing straights
- Lack basic bankroll management

The solution isn't better picks — it's **education first, community second, picks as validation.**

---

## Why We're Raising Prices to $50/Month

**Positioning requires pricing validation.** At current pricing, the offer looks "too good to be true" which undermines credibility and attracts only breakeven/small-bankroll players. Higher pricing signals:
- Real value in education, not just picks
- Serious players only (filters out noise)
- Validated methodology worth paying for

**Tension acknowledged:** This moves away from our core audience (low bankroll, mostly Flif users). But the math is clear: We need revenue to sustain, and education-first model attracts better lifetime value players. Goal isn't to abandon low-bankroll players—it's to help them think bigger long-term while we focus on sustainable growth.

### 2. **Redesign Education Stack** (Concurrent with EV)

**Core teaching moments:**

1. **Bankroll + Unit Sizing**
   - 1-3% unit size (not more)
   - Max 3-5 units per day
   - Unit size is your speed limiter

2. **Variance is Real**
   - Coin flip analogy: 90% win coin, but do you bet your whole bankroll on one flip?
   - One variance event can flip multiple legs in a parlay
   - Long-term mindset required (not "I missed tonight's game")
   - Build a responsible gaming channel

3. **Time-Value of Capital**
   - Research time spent = confidence level earned
   - Can only do X effective research in Y hours before market moves
   - Don't waste units on 30-min research when you can do 2-hour deep dives
   - Better to have fewer, higher-conviction bets

4. **Straights Over Parlays**
   - Variance compounds with legs (parlay loses on 1 leg, not 1 unit)
   - Better payout ≠ better EV
   - Exception: DFS apps force parlays but give better odds (acceptable)
   - Theme: variance management

5. **Live Betting Edge**
   - Example: Wait for Blackhawks to score, then take Hurricanes at better odds
   - Market is sharp for a reason—find better entry points
   - Wager-to-return ratio matters more than just odds
   - Think shoe reselling: price moves, find the arbitrage

6. **Variance Cap on Decision-Making**
   - Stick with a player/market for 10+ events before scaling
   - Jumping unit size creates drawdown vulnerability
   - Example: $25 → $100 units, lose first bet = already down 4 units on variance

**Where it lives:**
- Betting tips channel (strategy focus)
- New dedicated strategy resource
- Education-first onboarding flow
- Analyst call-outs on methodology
